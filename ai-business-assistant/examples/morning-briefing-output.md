# Example: Morning Briefing Output

This is what a real morning briefing looks like for a solo founder running an e-commerce business with one part-time assistant.

---

Good morning. Friday, April 18.

## Calendar (4 events)

**10:00 — Team sync with Lena (15 min)**
No prep needed. Weekly standup, she'll update on order fulfillment numbers.

**13:00 — Call with potential client: marketplace seller, 300 orders/day**
PREP: They asked about review automation last week. Pull up the demo video and the pricing doc. Key question to ask: how many reviews per day are negative? If under 20%, suggest starting with the review responder only. If over 20%, full package makes more sense.

**15:30 — Accountant call (30 min)**
PREP: Q1 tax summary is due. Check if Lena uploaded the March receipts to the shared folder. If not, ping her before the call.

**18:00 — Dentist**
Personal. Blocked.

## Emails (9 unread)

**Auto-reply drafted (4):**
- Supplier: invoice confirmation for April shipment. Draft: "Got it, payment goes out Monday."
- Newsletter subscription request from a marketing tool. Draft: unsubscribe link clicked.
- Meeting reschedule from Wednesday client. Draft: "Thursday 14:00 works. Confirmed."
- Shipping notification from vendor. No reply needed, logged.

**Needs you (3):**
- Client asking about pricing for a 3-agent package. Draft ready for your review. They're comparing with a competitor who quoted 50K rub. Our edge: we include 30 days of async support.
- Partner proposal from an n8n consulting firm. Complex, read yourself. 2 pages, mostly about revenue share on referrals.
- Tax accountant asking about a specific March expense (34,500 rub to "Cloud Services"). She needs the invoice. Check if it's the n8n hosting or the API credits.

**Spam/irrelevant (2):** filtered, no action.

## Top 3 priorities today

1. **Finish the client proposal for the marketplace seller** (deadline: before the 13:00 call). Estimated time: 45 min. This is the highest-value task today because closing this client = 30K rub recurring.

2. **Review and send the 3 email drafts** (15 min). Quick wins, clears the inbox before the weekend.

3. **Upload March receipts for accountant** (20 min, or delegate to Lena). Blocking the tax call at 15:30.
